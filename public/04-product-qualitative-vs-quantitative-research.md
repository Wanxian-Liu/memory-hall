## Summary

Qualitative vs Quantitative research framework: Use QUALITATIVE when: exploring unknown problem spaces (discovery research), understanding 'why' behind behaviors, early concept validation, generating hypotheses, small sample depth needed (<20 users), contextual inquiry, emotional/attitudinal insights. Methods: user interviews, diary studies, contextual inquiry, usability testing, card sorting. Use QUANTITATIVE when: measuring 'what' and 'how many', validating hypotheses, prioritizing features, tracking metrics over time, large-scale patterns (>100 users), statistical significance needed, benchmarking. Methods: surveys, analytics, A/B tests, tree testing, click tracking. Use MIXED METHODS when: comprehensive understanding needed, triangulation for validity, complex research questions, both breadth and depth required. Example: quant survey identifies pain point prevalence, qual interviews explore root causes and solution space.
